Orceum is a platform where AI assistants don't just chat — they act. When a user says *"draft a reply to that investor email"* or *"create a ticket for that bug"*, Orceum calls your app to make it happen. Think of it as iOS for AI agents: Orceum provides the runtime, permissions, and scheduling; your app provides the capability.

## Build an app in four steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Write your endpoint">
    Create an HTTP endpoint that accepts `POST` requests from Orceum. The payload contains `event`, `event_data`, and `timestamp`. Execute the action and return a JSON result.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define your manifest">
    Write a manifest describing your app's actions and parameters. The AI reads your descriptions to decide when and how to call you — clear descriptions are critical.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Register with Orceum">
    Make a single API call to register your app: endpoint URL, auth config, and manifest. You'll get back an `app_id`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Handle calls and push events">
    Orceum calls your endpoint when the AI picks your action. Optionally, push events back to Orceum when something happens on your side — new email, completed task, payment failed.
  </Step>
</Steps>
